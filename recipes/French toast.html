French toast

description

French toast is a classic breakfast dish made by soaking slices of bread in a mixture of beaten eggs, milk, and sometimes sugar and vanilla, then frying them in a pan until golden brown. The result is a warm, slightly crispy exterior with a soft and custardy interior. It is often served with toppings like syrup, powdered sugar, fresh fruits, or whipped cream, making it a sweet and indulgent breakfast option.

The origins of French toast date back centuries, with versions of the dish found in many cultures. In some variations, it’s made with stale bread to prevent waste, transforming leftovers into a delicious meal. While traditionally a sweet dish, savory versions can also be made by omitting sugar and adding ingredients like cheese or herbs. French toast’s versatility and ease of preparation have made it a beloved breakfast choice around the world.


ingredients

  • ⅔ cup milk
  • 2 large eggs
  • 1 teaspoon vanilla extract (Optional)
  • ¼ teaspoon ground cinnamon (Optional)
  • salt to taste
  • 6 thick slices bread
  • 1 tablespoon unsalted butter, or more as needed

Steps

  1. Gather all ingredients
  2. Whisk milk, eggs, vanilla, cinnamon, and salt together in a shallow bowl.
  3. Lightly butter a griddle or skillet and heat over medium-high heat. Dunk bread in the egg mixture, soaking both sides.
  4. Transfer to the hot skillet and cook until golden, 3 to 4 minutes per side.
  5. Serve hot.
